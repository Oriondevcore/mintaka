Infrastructural Connectivity and Transport Challenges
A critical factor for any establishment in the eThekwini South region is the status of transport infrastructure. The Zar Hotel is located in an area that has historically relied on the Metrorail Southern Coast Line for commuter and tourist movement. However, as of March 2026, the rail environment in the Durban Metropolitan area remains a significant challenge for the tourism sector.
The Status of the Metrorail Southern Coast Line (March 2026)
The Durban Metrorail service experienced a systemic collapse between 2019 and 2022, largely due to a combination of the COVID-19 pandemic, cable theft, and vandalism.[13, 14] While the Passenger Rail Agency of South Africa (PRASA) has committed over R622 million to infrastructure upgrades, the recovery remains uneven.
Key findings regarding the rail environment include:
Partial Service: The Southern Coast Line operates only partial services. In many instances, the line terminates at Isipingo, leaving stations like Doonside (which is only 460-470 meters from the hotel) with unreliable or non-existent service.[9, 14, 15]
Operational Inefficiency: Due to the degraded condition of tracks and signaling equipment, trains that are operational run at significantly reduced speeds. A journey that would take 30 minutes by car can take up to four times longer via rail.[14]
Infrastructure Decay: Stations often lack functional digital display boards and automated access gates. Furthermore, ticketing systems remain predominantly cash-based, as the rollout of card machines has faced administrative delays.[14]
Safety Concerns: While PRASA has deployed security guards on platforms and trains, frequent "system issues" and mid-track stops create a perception of unreliability that deters leisure travelers.[14]
Given these conditions, the Zar Hotel’s provision of its own shuttle service and its proximity to major road arteries like the N2 highway are crucial for guest mobility.
